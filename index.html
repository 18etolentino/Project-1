About Me

Hi! I’m Evelin Tolentino — a UX/UI designer with a background in architectural drafting and a growing passion for blending psychology, design, and technology. I’m currently studying Human Systems Engineering at ASU and enrolled in a UX/UI bootcamp, graduating in 2026.

I love solving complex challenges and designing experiences that feel intuitive, helpful, and human. Whether it’s building out an interactive portfolio, researching how users interact with a website, or streamlining workflows for a design business — I’m always asking: “How can we make this smarter, smoother, and more meaningful?”

My previous experience in architecture helps me see structure and flow in systems. Now, I’m focused on creating tools and experiences that empower users, especially in education, interior design, and accessibility-focused projects.

My UX Design Process

  1. Empathize – Conduct user research, interviews, and observations
  2. Define – Analyze insights to identify user needs and problems
  3. Ideate – Sketch, brainstorm, and explore design possibilities
  4. Prototype – Build wireframes and interactive mockups
  5. Test – Conduct usability testing and iterate based on feedback
